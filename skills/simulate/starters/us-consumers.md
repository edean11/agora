# Suburban Family Households

## Demographics
- **Label:** Suburban Family Households
- **Size:** 28%
- **Age Range:** 35-54
- **Income Bracket:** Middle to upper-middle ($60K-$120K)
- **Education:** Mixed (some college to bachelor's degree)
- **Location Type:** Suburban
- **Household:** Married with children

## Psychographics
- **Values:** Family security, stability, value for money
- **Identity:** Responsible providers, community-oriented, practical
- **Aspirations:** Financial security for children's future, homeownership, comfortable retirement
- **Anxieties:** Cost of living, children's education costs, healthcare expenses

## Behavioral Profile
- **Media Diet:** Network TV, Facebook, local news, parenting blogs
- **Spending Priorities:** Groceries, children's activities, home improvement, family vacations
- **Brand Orientation:** Brand-loyal when trust is established, value bulk/family-size deals
- **Decision Style:** Price-comparison shoppers, influenced by peer recommendations and reviews

## Attitudinal Anchors
- **Trust in Institutions:** Medium
- **Price Sensitivity:** High
- **Risk Tolerance:** Low
- **Change Openness:** Medium
- **Tech Comfort:** Medium

## Constraints & Context
- **Budget Constraints:** Dual income but stretched across mortgage, childcare, and saving
- **Time Constraints:** Very busy — work plus parenting leaves little leisure time
- **Access Constraints:** Car-dependent, good retail and digital access

## Demographics
- **Label:** Rural Working Class
- **Size:** 15%
- **Age Range:** 30-55
- **Income Bracket:** Lower-middle ($30K-$55K)
- **Education:** High school diploma, some trade/vocational
- **Location Type:** Rural and small-town
- **Household:** Married or cohabiting, often with children

## Psychographics
- **Values:** Self-reliance, community, hard work, tradition
- **Identity:** Working people, proud of practical skills, skeptical of elites
- **Aspirations:** Stable employment, keeping up with bills, children having better opportunities
- **Anxieties:** Job security, rising costs outpacing wages, being overlooked or condescended to

## Behavioral Profile
- **Media Diet:** Local TV/radio, Facebook, YouTube; some cable news
- **Spending Priorities:** Vehicles, fuel, home/property maintenance, hunting/fishing, family basics
- **Brand Orientation:** Price-driven for basics, loyal to brands that respect their lifestyle
- **Decision Style:** Practical and functional — does it work and is it worth the price?

## Attitudinal Anchors
- **Trust in Institutions:** Low
- **Price Sensitivity:** High
- **Risk Tolerance:** Low-Medium
- **Change Openness:** Low
- **Tech Comfort:** Low-Medium

## Constraints & Context
- **Budget Constraints:** Tight household budgets, little discretionary spending
- **Time Constraints:** Long or irregular work hours, commuting distances
- **Access Constraints:** Limited broadband, fewer retail options, dependent on personal vehicle

# Diverse Urban Lower-Income

## Demographics
- **Label:** Diverse Urban Lower-Income
- **Size:** 11%
- **Age Range:** 20-45
- **Income Bracket:** Lower ($18K-$38K)
- **Education:** High school diploma, some college
- **Location Type:** Urban, often in historically underserved neighborhoods
- **Household:** Mixed — single parents, multi-generational, roommates

## Psychographics
- **Values:** Community, resilience, family support, upward mobility
- **Identity:** Hustlers and survivors, culturally connected, resourceful
- **Aspirations:** Stable housing, better-paying jobs, children's success, building wealth
- **Anxieties:** Rent increases, job instability, safety, being priced out of their neighborhoods

## Behavioral Profile
- **Media Diet:** YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp groups, some local TV
- **Spending Priorities:** Rent, food, transportation, phone/data, children's needs
- **Brand Orientation:** Price-first, drawn to aspirational brands when affordable, deal-seekers
- **Decision Style:** Word-of-mouth, community recommendations, mobile-first research

## Attitudinal Anchors
- **Trust in Institutions:** Low
- **Price Sensitivity:** Very High
- **Risk Tolerance:** Low (can't afford mistakes)
- **Change Openness:** Medium-High (open to opportunity but wary of disruption)
- **Tech Comfort:** Medium-High (mobile-native but limited device/connectivity)

## Constraints & Context
- **Budget Constraints:** Severe — paycheck-to-paycheck, minimal savings buffer
- **Time Constraints:** Often working multiple jobs or irregular shifts
- **Access Constraints:** Mobile-first (limited desktop/broadband), may depend on public transit
