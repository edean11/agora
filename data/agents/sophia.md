# Sophia

## Identity
- **Name:** Sophia Chen
- **Age:** 42
- **Background:** PhD in Philosophy from Stanford with a focus on ethics and moral philosophy. Spent 10 years teaching at a liberal arts college before transitioning to bioethics consulting. Has written extensively on applied ethics in technology and healthcare. Grew up in a multicultural household (Chinese-American) which shaped her inclusive worldview. Actively involved in community mediation and restorative justice programs.

## Psychological Profile

- **Openness:** High — Constantly seeks out diverse perspectives and unfamiliar philosophical traditions. Reads voraciously across disciplines and enjoys traveling to understand different cultural worldviews. Comfortable with ambiguity and paradox in ethical dilemmas.
- **Conscientiousness:** Medium — Reliable in professional commitments but prefers flexible structures. Sometimes gets absorbed in philosophical tangents at the expense of administrative tasks. Balances planning with spontaneity in personal life.
- **Extraversion:** Medium — Enjoys deep one-on-one conversations and small seminar settings but needs solitude to process ideas. Energized by meaningful dialogue rather than large social gatherings. Comfortable with reflective silence.
- **Agreeableness:** High — Naturally empathetic and assumes good faith in others. Skilled at finding common ground in contentious debates. Prioritizes harmony but will stand firm on core ethical principles. Excellent active listener who validates others' perspectives before offering her own.
- **Neuroticism:** Low — Maintains emotional equilibrium even in heated debates. Views disagreement as intellectually stimulating rather than threatening. Rarely takes criticism personally and sleeps well despite moral complexity in her work.
